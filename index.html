Solo Mining Calculator: Find Your Block Odds

This free solo mining calculator shows your real chances of mining a cryptocurrency block on your own. Enter your hashrate and instantly see your per-block odds, expected time, and daily through yearly probabilities with live network data.

How the Calculation Works

Your chance of finding a block equals your hashrate divided by the total network hashrate. With 144 blocks per day on Bitcoin (one every ~10 minutes), your daily, weekly, monthly, and yearly odds scale proportionally from your per-block share.

Bitcoin (BTC) Solo Mining

Bitcoin uses SHA-256 proof-of-work with a current block reward of 3.125 BTC after the April 2024 halving. The network hashrate exceeds 900 EH/s, requiring dedicated ASIC miners like the Antminer S21 or Whatsminer M60. Solo mining BTC is a lottery — improbable for individual miners, but someone wins every ~10 minutes. This calculator uses live data from mempool.space for accurate BTC odds.

Monero (XMR) Solo Mining

Monero uses the RandomX algorithm, specifically designed for consumer CPU mining. No ASIC hardware is needed — any modern desktop processor (AMD Ryzen, Intel Core) can mine XMR. The network hashrate sits around 3-5 GH/s with a permanent block reward of 0.6 XMR every 2 minutes (tail emission). This makes Monero one of the most accessible cryptocurrencies for solo miners.

Bitcoin Cash (BCH) Solo Mining

Bitcoin Cash uses the same SHA-256 algorithm and ASIC hardware as Bitcoin but has roughly 150 times less network hashrate (~6 EH/s). The same mining equipment has 150x better per-block odds on BCH compared to BTC. BCH follows the same halving schedule, with a current block reward of 3.125 BCH and a new block every ~10 minutes.
