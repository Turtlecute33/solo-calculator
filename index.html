Bitcoin (BTC) Solo Mining Calculator

Calculate your odds of solo mining a Bitcoin block. Bitcoin uses SHA-256 proof-of-work with a current block reward of 3.125 BTC after the April 2024 halving. The network hashrate exceeds 900 EH/s, requiring dedicated ASIC miners like the Antminer S21 or Whatsminer M60.

How BTC Solo Mining Odds Work

Your chance of finding a block starts with your hashrate divided by the total network hashrate. This calculator then converts that expected block rate into an exact probability of finding at least one block over 1 day, 1 week, 1 month, or 1 year using a Poisson model. Solo mining BTC is still a lottery — improbable for individual miners, but someone wins every ~10 minutes.

Monero (XMR) Solo Mining Calculator

Calculate your odds of solo mining a Monero block. Monero uses the RandomX algorithm, specifically designed for consumer CPU mining. No ASIC hardware is needed — any modern desktop processor (AMD Ryzen, Intel Core) can mine XMR.

How XMR Solo Mining Odds Work

The Monero network hashrate sits around 3-5 GH/s with a permanent block reward of 0.6 XMR every 2 minutes (tail emission). Your chance per block equals your CPU hashrate divided by the network total. This makes Monero one of the most accessible cryptocurrencies for solo miners using consumer hardware.

Bitcoin Cash (BCH) Solo Mining Calculator

Calculate your odds of solo mining a Bitcoin Cash block. BCH uses the same SHA-256 algorithm and ASIC hardware as Bitcoin but has roughly 150 times less network hashrate (~6 EH/s), giving the same equipment 150x better per-block odds.

How BCH Solo Mining Odds Work

Bitcoin Cash follows the same halving schedule as Bitcoin, with a current block reward of 3.125 BCH and a new block every ~10 minutes. The calculator estimates the expected number of blocks your hashrate would find, then converts that into a time-based probability that stays stable even for very large inputs.
